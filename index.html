About

I am a disease ecologist and quantitative scientist with over a decade of experience working at the interface of wildlife ecology, infectious disease, and public health — a field increasingly known as One Health.

My research spans mathematical and statistical modelling, Bayesian inference, and evidence synthesis, applied to questions ranging from the ecological drivers of zoonotic disease to the population-level impacts of policy interventions. I have worked with the Natural History Museum, the Royal Veterinary College, and the UK Government's Joint Biosecurity Centre, and have published in journals including The Lancet, PNAS Nexus, and Trends in Parasitology.

Alongside my scientific work, I have a deep commitment to the natural world — most recently expressed in hiking New Zealand's 3,000 km Te Araroa trail. This grounds my consulting in a genuine passion for ecology, biodiversity, and the environments I work to understand and protect.

I am based in the Cotswolds and work remotely with clients across the UK and internationally.

Selected projects

Work

A selection of recent consulting and policy-relevant research engagements.

Soil Association & British Ecological Society

2025

Public health implications of organic agriculture

Independently designed and led a rapid evidence review for the Soil Association, examining the public health case for organic farming across key policy-relevant areas including foodborne disease and antimicrobial resistance. Synthesised a complex and contested evidence base into a structured report with clear conclusions and recommendations, used by the client in internal strategy and external communications.

Joint Biosecurity Centre, DHSC

2020–2021

COVID-19 vaccination impact modelling

Embedded analyst at the UK Government's Joint Biosecurity Centre during the COVID-19 pandemic. Designed and implemented a mathematical model projecting the impact of vaccination on cases, hospital admissions, and deaths — prior to widespread vaccine availability. Outputs were used directly by senior government officials to inform policy. Summarised SPI-M findings for ministerial briefings and published a peer-reviewed paper on COVID-19 immunity in The Lancet Respiratory Medicine.

Royal Veterinary College / FibroScHot Consortium

2022–2024

Mathematical modelling of schistosomiasis control policy

Led development of policy-relevant transmission models for a randomised controlled trial on schistosomiasis control in Uganda, in collaboration with the Ugandan Ministry of Health. Used Bayesian inference to estimate model parameters and simulate the impact of treatment policies on infection and disease outcomes. Results contributed to a Policy Brief on control in disease hotspots and a first-author paper in PNAS Nexus.
